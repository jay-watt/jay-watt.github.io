PROBLEM SOLVING

A time I was blocked on a simple problem.

A simple problem that comes up for me a lot is losing my glasses. When this happens, I run through several problem-solving techniques. Similar to writing pseudocode, I will break down my movements, since the last time I saw them, step-by-step. Once I have identified these steps, I can check each place methodically. Next, I try the rubber ducky method during which I talk about out loud where I would put them if I were me. It can also give me ideas on where to look by explaining out loud why I may have lost them, e.g. I had a deadline or was hungry. If I canâ€™t track them down after these two steps, I ask my peers, generally my flatmates, or whoever I am with at the time. My stress levels often increase proportionately with the time I spend looking. Unfortunately, this makes me less likely to find them as I struggle to be methodical when I am stressed. As a result, the last problem solving method is usually the most successful. When someone with fresh eyes is able to locate the glasses with ease, I am always reminded that the people around me are valuable resources I am able to learn from and that we are more capable together than apart.

A time I elegantly solved a problem.

This week, one of my lamps broke. The wiring was fine, but the part that the lightbulb socket screws into has threading that is now too worn to work. This meant the bulb was hanging from the lamp but was still able to be turned on. I ended up attaching a rubber band from the frame to the socket, temporarily fixing the socket in its holder. This avoided the need for glue or any other materials that could permanently mark the lamp, while giving it full functionality. I was happy with this solution as it gave me the option to implement a permanent fix (i.e. getting a new part) in my own time, while still having the ability to use the lamp. The problem solving technique I used was trying something, definitely my go-to technique. While it is sometimes inefficient due to the inevitable process difficult to visualise initially. I felt engaged throughout the process and happy that I could be resourceful with items within arms reach. I learnt that it is very handy to have small multipurpose items close by in spaces I spend a lot of time, e.g. rubber band, paper clip, safety pin, etc.

Rubber ducky method medium medium I definitely appreciate how useful it can be to break a code problem down into normal everyday language. This is a technique I use when pseudocode is not sufficient and I do not have access to a person to talk to.

Console.logging high high I love console logging. I find it very useful to implement as I go at each step so that I can be sure where bugs are located.

However, because of the range of answers available, if I am unable to find information relating to the specific problem I am having, I can usually at least find resources that are related enough to help me gain fresh perspective on the problem.
