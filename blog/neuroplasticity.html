NEUROPLASTICITY & GROWTH MINDSET

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people

Understanding neuroplasticity can be simplified to the basic concept that the brain is a dynamic and responsive organ that is constantly capable of change. Learning that each thought changes the structure and function of your brain by affecting the neurons at the microscopic level, empowers people to be more aware of how their time is spent and its impact.

“The world I create is always creating me back.”

With continuous stimulus (e.g. an emotion, a feeling, a behavior, a habit, an exercise, an experience), the repetition of the stimulus will cause neurogenesis (the creation of new neurons). It is useful to understand this process of learning in relationship with synaptic pruning, the process by which the neurons lose synapses and surface area through underuse. In simple terms, we have the power to make thought processes stronger and easier to access, or weaker and less intrusive.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

Repeatedly practicing an activity or accessing a memory will cause neural networks (groups of neurons that fire together) to create electrochemical pathways. This means they will shape themselves according to that activity or memory.

Also, performing tasks or recalling information that causes different neurons to fire in concert, it will strengthen the connections between those cells. For example, learning a new skill that relates to previous life experience or skills that have already been practiced long-term.

Discuss what a growth mindset is and why it is relevant

A growth mindset describes the extent to which individuals believe they can change and grow. That is, someone with a growth mindset believes that something they cannot achieve today, may be achievable tomorrow.

It is relevant because it dictates a person's willingness to take on challenges and new experiences. Someone with a growth mindset is more likely to step outside of their comfort zone, as they understand that perseverance is more valuable in the long run than a standalone result of a singular challenge. They will also be more motivated to dedicate time and make sacrifices to benefit performance in the future, as well as to help others grow. With a growth mindset, higher self esteem can be achieved by allowing oneself room to improve, to make mistakes and to learn from failures.

By avoiding giving my selective memory the power to shape my view of myself and my skills, I can reduce the impact of individual experiences on my big picture perspective. I want to avoid having tunnel vision in cases of both success and failure and see them as part of a larger journey.
