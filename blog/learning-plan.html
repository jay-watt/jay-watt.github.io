What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

My long term programming goals have not shifted since completing foundations. I would like to work towards projects relating to two key areas: sustainable waste management and Pasifika knowledge accessibility. I would prefer to work freelance on fixed-term contracts as I enjoy being fully committed to projects then having periods of rest and reflection.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

My biggest strengths will be my work ethic, effective strategies to stay focussed and on-task, and experience in working in a team. While I have found reaching out to peers intimidating throughout foundations, I have achieved the best results in team environments and genuinely acknowledge the importance of learning through collaborating. My biggest limitation will be the amount of energy it takes for me to effectively communicate with others, as I struggle with being present, particularly in regards to actively listening. While I have personal and interpersonal strategies for mitigating this, it is essential I get adequate time to myself in order to maintain self-awareness. Beyond rest, my best tool I have to cope with this difficulty is being honest about my weaknesses in order to compromise with those I am working with.

LEARNING PLAN

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

I would like to work freelance on projects that I am invested in. At the moment, there are two main areas I would like to aim towards working in. One is sustainable waste management. Some apps/websites that are lacking in this sector focus on goals like public education, creating connections between people with waste and community members with recycling solutions, waste worker job assistance, etc.

The other area would be Pasifika knowledge access as I feel there is a lack of well-designed apps and websites available to research my culture. While the most important way is engaging with the elders I have around me, I feel strongly about there being other routes to connection, particularly for those in the diaspora.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

My major strength in learning is creative problem solving. Many of the skills I have and cherish today were learnt outside of formal learning environments and I recognise that valuable knowledge can often be found in unexpected places. I have also always enjoyed working and collaborating in DIY environments, and pride myself on being resourceful.

My biggest limitation is getting stuck and struggling to move on. It has been helpful to get into the habit of using a pomodoro break timer but I will need to keep it up long term in order to retain meaningful changes. Another difficulty I have is balancing my learning with my other obligations.

My priority, whether I am studying or working, is my family. They are consistently supportive and understanding of my other commitments, but I often struggle to acknowledge my limited capacity when I have demanding workloads

What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I recognise the importance of reflecting throughout project work and learning. I would like to practise this more throughout my time at DA so that I can identify the areas that are useful for me to reflect on. By the end of the course, I want to have a clear set of questions that are productive for me to ask myself while learning.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme
