Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

My family has shaped my values and given me guidance in identifying my long-term fulfilling priorities. I am still learning that I have the tools and skills I need to fulfill my goals, passed down from my ancestors and taught to me by those whose lifetimes have overlapped with mine.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

My strengths are compassion towards others and having a grounding family. This combination increases my patience towards others and gives me perspective. Additionally, my experience doing physical team-based work in dangerous environments has honed my effective communication skills.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

I have been consistently unsuccessful in tertiary learning environments. While there are valid reasons, I struggle with self-confidence and self-belief. I think DA will challenge me to push past my imposter syndrome, particularly when it comes time to look for work. I have been most comfortable in physical jobs the past several years so I am apprehensive about fitting into an office environment.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

In my time working at the Happy Valley Tip in Te Whanganui-a-Tara, the team was very diverse in terms of work and life experience. Many staff members had been there long-term from contrasting previous careers and there were often differing opinions on the most effective way to complete tasks. When I held a supervisor role there, I focussed on listening to team members in order to assign tasks that worked to people's strengths. I also found that involving team-members in long-term planning to a higher degree provided bigger picture perspective, as well as increasing the quality of these plans through collaboration. The main thing I learnt is that while there may be difficulties in reaching a shared consensus, it is the differences in skillsets and experiences that gave the team its effectiveness and strength.
